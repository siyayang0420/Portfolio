CourtOn is a mobile application created by students at the British Columbia Institute of Technology. The objective of the app is to create a platform for badminton players to host a game with a self-created group or to join other organizer’s groups at any of the badminton centres in Vancouver’s Lower-mainland.

Idea Solution

CourtOn focuses on managing and creating groups that users can be called “organizers” to host badminton games with other players. The organizer can choose a badminton centre, date, hours and which badminton court. The organizer would need to contact the badminton centre to know which courts are available that day. There will be no centre managers involved, so it will not connect the information to the centre’s database. The main focus is on groups, bringing the badminton community closer, having a sense of belonging and building relationships through communication in the app.

The group(s) you create is a great way to play with your own friends or new friends. As a user, you can also join groups by other organizers and become a member of the group. By becoming a member of the group you joined, you can view the group’s information, comment or leave the group. All your activity can be viewed through your personal profile page.



Techinal Solution

CourtOn is built by using react-native, and it is build for IOS. We use Henry Leung(D3 Program Instructor)'s api and postgresSQL, and host on Heroku.

Research for the Initial Idea
The present registration system at the 6 main badminton centres in Vancouver requires a manually handwritten signup sheet at the reception desk. In order to book a court for each of the 6 badminton centres, users can only call or email them for inquiries. Sometimes the staff members do not answer the phone calls or respond to emails. Some websites of the centres have schedules displayed, however, users have to click on the event slot on the calendar one by one to know the details.

There are a few managers from the different badminton centres that do not try to engage with their customers online or even in reality. As a team member for this project is an active and avid badminton player, they had recognized the disconnection between the players and the badminton centre’s social environment.

Solution
To connect and centralize the gap between badminton players and the badminton centres/community in Vancouver, the idea of creating a platform to provide an online convenient court booking and payment process was formed.

CourtOn will cater to the niche market of the badminton community in Vancouver. Within seconds, the user will be able to reserve and pay for drop-in spots and rent courts of a particular badminton centre online. These reservations will be based on the available time and date slot as well as the specific location and court they want to play at. Users of CourtOn can modify or cancel their booking(s) of their session and the changes will update the manager’s side in real-time.

As well as a live-updated schedule for every booking will take place. A “News” section will be the connection between the players and the badminton centre’s organization as it will provide updates and information about upcoming events/tournaments.
